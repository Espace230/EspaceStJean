CELEBRATE THE CLOSING OF ‘SOUL & CITYSCAPES’ WITH ARTIST TALKS AT ESPACE ST-JEAN

Experience an immersive journey through identity, place and texture.

Montreal, Tuesday October 8, 2024 — As the successful exhibitions Soul & Cityscapes and Abstraction non-conventionnelle come to a close, Espace St-Jean invites art lovers to celebrate with a special event featuring artists Denise Buisman Pilger and Louis-Bernard St-Jean, who will be in attendance for a series of artist talks throughout the day.

Join us on Saturday, October 19th, from 12 p.m. to 5 p.m. at Espace St-Jean in the Belgo Building (372, Sainte-Catherine St. W, suite 521) for an inspiring and intimate day with the artists!

Throughout the day, the artists will hold several short talks, offering unique insights into their creative processes, inspirations, and the stories behind the artworks in the exhibition. Attendees will have the opportunity to engage with both the personal and global themes explored in the works, diving deeper into the intersections of self and surroundings that define Soul & Cityscapes with Denise and exploring the splendors of nature that inspire Louis-Bernard’s series of sculptural oil paintings.

About the works presented

Denise Buisman Pilger's series, Unveiling, presents an introspective exploration of identity through abstract mixed media paintings. By layering acrylics, washi paper, and oil pastels, Buisman-Pilger crafts a visual narrative about the challenges of staying true to oneself amid societal pressures. HerTraveling the World series, on the other hand, takes viewers on a journey through urban landscapes from around the globe, blending photography and painting to reflect the richness and complexity of city life.

Louis-Bernard St-Jean’s bold, impressionistic works are characterized by heavily textured, organic patterns, which bring an incredible depth, structure, and sense of movement to the conceptual experience, inviting viewers to engage with the artwork on both a visual and sensory level.


“I’m really looking forward to sharing the inspiration behind my work and diving deeper into the stories that shaped these pieces. It’s a wonderful opportunity to connect with visitors on a more personal level,” says Denise Buisman Pilger.

About the Artist

Born in Amsterdam, Pilger has showcased her art internationally, from Art Basel Miami to exhibitions in New York, Paris, Japan, and across Canada. Her cross-cultural life experiences are deeply embedded in her work, inviting viewers to reflect on both the personal and collective human experience.

Don’t miss this final chance to experience Soul & Cityscapes and celebrate the closing of this remarkable exhibition with the artists themselves!
